Dining at IC

Dining at school can take some getting used to. Balancing your schedule with your dining hall's schedule can be a challenge. And nothing is worse than going hungry because you were five minutes late for a meal. But for a little bit of help, look not further! Here, you can find information such as the IC dining hall's meal schedule, other on-campus options, and even a few off-campus dining recomendations. Whether you're always on time, you've just missed a meal, or you're looking for something different, we've got you covered.

College Ave. Subs

College Ave. Subs is a great place to pick up a great sandwitch on campus. Its hours are limited, but if you're free for lunch (and you don't mind a long line) you should stop by and give it a try.

  • Monday-Friday: 11:00am-3:00pm
  • Saturday & Sunday: Closed

C-Store

The C-Store is a great option for busy students. Maybe you missed a meal at the dining hall, or maybe you're too busy studying to sit and eat for an hour. The C-Store is the place to pick up some grab and go provisions or a quick pack of ramen noodels.

  • Monday-Friday: 7:00am-9:00pm
  • Saturday & Sunday: Closed

Starbuck's in Lincoln Hall

Who doesn't love Starbuck's? Whether you need some help waking up, a quick bite between classes, a boost before some late night studying, or just a coffee with friends, Starbuck's has you covered.

Off Campus Recomendations

Burger Board

As far as off-campus dining, Burger Board is a no brainer. Boasting a charming environment, handmade milkshakes, sundays, and concretes, and the best burgers in America (according to one appreciative customer's review) burger board is the perfect stop if you're looking for some classic smashburgers made right to order. Some menu recomendations include the burgers (of course) with whatever toppings you like, cheese curds, a midwest staple side, a horse shoe, sort of a regional delicacy, and a cherry marshmallow milkshake.

Leo's Pizza

Leo's Pizza has been a staple in the community for decades. Family owned and operated since day one, Leo's pizza is known for serving up delicious tavern style and pan pizza as well as other Italian favorites. Next time you're looking for better pizza than you can get at the dining hall, consider calling Leo's pizza for better pizza than you can get at Papa John's. A few menu recomendations include the pizza (of course) with your favorite toppings, garlic bread, a cheap and delicious option for an appetizer, side, or snack, and Bosco sticks, cheese filled bread sticks seasoned and cooked to perfection and served alongside Leo's homemade marinara sauce.
